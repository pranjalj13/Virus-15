The ENIAC Club

The official computer club of Bluebells School International which has been initiated by the students is also run by us. Originally, ENIAC means Electronic Numerator Integrator and Calculator, but this word means much more to us. According to us, it's full form is "Encoding. Networking. Innovating. Achieving. Computing." Overall, a high octane filled adrenaline rush powered by the brains of the members brings this club to existence. Our previous events, VIRUS 2001, 2003 and Enter-o-Virus 2005, 2006, 2007, 2008, and 2009 were a roaring success thanks to all the participating teams. Our school has also been awarded the prestigious Intel Award for the best Integration of Technology with Education and British Council International School Award.

Theme

THEME

The theme for Virus 2014 is 2.5D. Technically, 2.5D means pseudo 3D or ¾ perspective. Usually described 2D graphical projections and similar techniques used to cause a series of images (or scenes) to simulate the appearance of being three-dimensional (3D) when in fact they are not. However, we believe that there is more to it. It is a different way to look at things. A simpler, yet effective way to combine the 3D world and the 2D world. A concept to bridge the gap between the real and the virtual world.

Events

PROGRAMMING

Number of participants: 2
Software: C++
Time Allowed: 120 Minutes
Eligibility: XI‐XII

You will be provided with a computer, C++, TURBO compiler and a set of four tasks. There will be a 30 minutes objective type preliminary round to find the finalist among you. Prelim round would have a MCQ round, one word answer round and an output finding round, participants are requested to bring HB pencil set with eraser.

MOVIE PRESENTATION

The participants are requested to make a movie on the topics given below and present it to the judges on the day of the event. The movie should be of at least 3 minutes and should not exceed 4 minutes. Plagiarism will lead to disqualification.

The topics are:
1. Technology has loosened familial ties
2. Media has been responsible for increased crime rates against women
3. The new age woman believes in breaking stereotypes
4. Heritage preservation should be a national movement in India

QUIZ

Number of participants: 2

Eligibility: Open

The 30 minutes objective type prelims round will shortlist 6 teams for the final on‐stage round.

POWERPOINT

Number of Team: 1
Number of participants: 2
Time Allowed: 45 Minutes
Eligibility: Class V and below

Participants will be asked to use Microsoft PowerPoint to design and prepare a presentation. The topic along with a few text, images and Audio and Video files related to that topic will be given on the spot. No external material will be allowed.

RUBIK's CUBE

Number of participants: 3
Eligibility: Open
Maximum time per solve: 5 minutes (3x3x3) || 5 minutes (Pyraminx)

The event will consist of 2 rounds. The winners will be based on the average time of all the three rounds. Competitors are requested to bring their own cubes. Cubes will not be provided on the spot. Cubes with 6 distinct colors and of size 3x3x3 and Pyraminx will only be permitted. ￼￼￼￼ ￼Note: The 3x3x3 competition and Pyraminx competitions are independent of each other.

GAMING

Number of participants: 1

Eligibility: Open

Type: Console Gaming

Rules will be intimated to the participants on the spot. Participants must be prepared to face any kind of game.

DIGITAL IMAGING

Number of teams: 1
Number of Participants: 2
Time Allotted: 60 Minutes
Software: Adobe Photoshop CS6
Eligibility: Open

This challenging event encompasses the skills required in the field of photo editing. A few photos will be given on the spot. No external material will be allowed to add or use. Topics will be given on the spot ￼￼￼￼ ￼Entries will be judged on the basis of originality, creativity, code, functionality and aesthetics.

RULES

1. To be eligible for the winning trophy the school must participate in at least 5 out of 8 events.

2. In case of a tie for overall position the team which has positions in more number of events shall get the trophy.

3. All participants and guests are requested to arrive at the venue by latest 8:30 AM. Schools coming in late will not be given any extra time for the events they miss.

4. Each school can submit only one entry per event.

5. Schools are requested to confirm the details of the participation by completing the online Google Form latest by 24th July, 2013.

6. Participants of Movie Presentation should send the video by latest 30th July and are also requested to bring a copy on the final day.

7. Only the first 20 schools to register will be allowed to participate in the events, so register as soon as possible.
